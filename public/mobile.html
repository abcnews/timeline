Timelines

Wed 11 Oct 2017, 12:12 PM AEDT

This is a template article demonstrating how to turn a standard CoreMedia article into a timeline.

If you want to use this template:

  1. Open it in CoreMedia Explorer
  2. Copy and paste it into your work folder

It's worth noting as you begin the process of creating a timeline that you will need a specific date for each entry in your timeline, in order for this template to work.

Another thing worth noting here: any text above the first Heading 1 in your article will not be included in the timeline. It will appear above the timeline navigation widget.

Timeline sections

January 1, 2000

Timeline sections are delimited by Heading 1 sub-headings within the article which should be a brief headline description of what happened at that point in the timeline.

At some point before the next Heading 1 there should be a Heading 2 which specifies the date at which the timeline event occurs. Ideally, this Heading 2 would appear directly below the Heading 1.

Date formats

January 1, 2008

Quite a few different date formats are accepted, the key requirement being that they must be unambiguous. For example "10 January, 2008" is unambiguous whereas "10/1/2008" is ambiguous (this could be 10 Jan or 1 Oct depending on where you are in the world) and may not be the expected date for all users.

Some example date formats that definitely will work are:

  • January 10, 2008
  • 10 January, 2008
  • 10 January 2008
  • Thursday January 10th 2008
  • Jan 10th 2008

Not all formats you might expect to work will work (e.g. 10th January 2008) so if you're using date formats different from the list above, ensure everything is in order before publishing.

At this point, inexact dates are not supported. For example "January 2008" or "Late March 2015" will not work as they don't contain enough information to construct a specific date.

Years should always be four digits.

Please keep in mind: a single timeline should use one standard date format, even though the timeline tool itself supports different formats. The ABC News style is "January 10, 2008", so this should be your go-to format.

Times

1pm January 1, 2015

Date formats can include the time of day if this level of precision is important. Supported formats:

  • 1pm
  • 1:30pm
  • 1:30:45pm
  • 1:30

For times with an hour less than or equal to 12 but without an 'am' or 'pm' designator 'am' will be assumed.

Relative dates

February 2

It's possible to use dates without a year. When this is the case, the year is assumed to be the same as the date of the article. So this section will appear on the timeline at February 2nd on whatever the current year is (since this article is-or should be-unpublished).

Other content

March 1, 2015

There isn't any reason you can't use all the other types of content you'd ordinarily embed in an article like images, quotes, teasers, etc.

However, be careful with left and right embedded items as they may cause layout issues depending on the length of the timeline section compared to the height of the item. Make sure everything looks ship shape in preview before publishing.

What if I'm having trouble finding specific dates?

July 24, 2015

It is usually possible to find a specific date related to an event, even if you need to narrow the focus a little and choose a key date from within a wider timeframe. You can then write the wider timeframe into the accompanying text entry.

For example, instead of:

1939-1945

World War II

World War II was a global war that involved the vast majority of the world’s nations. The war began after Germany invaded Poland in September 1939, prompting France and the United Kingdom to declare war on Germany.

You could use…

September 1, 1939

World War II begins

Germany invaded Poland in September 1939, prompting France and the United Kingdom to declare war on Germany, events that led to a global conflict running until 1945 and involving the vast majority of the world’s nations.

Future dates

March 1, 2030

Finally, there isn't any reason you can't specify future dates.

After the timeline

If for any reason you need to add content below the timeline in your story, you need to insert a paragraph containing only '#endtimeline'. Anything after that will be outside the timeline.
